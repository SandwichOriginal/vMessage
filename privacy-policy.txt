  Last updated: March 2026

1. OVERVIEW
----------------------------------------------------------------
vMessage is designed with privacy as a core principle. This
policy explains what data is and is not collected when you use
the Service.

Short version: we collect almost nothing, and what little
passes through third-party infrastructure is outside our
control and is not stored by us.


2. DATA WE DO NOT COLLECT
----------------------------------------------------------------
vMessage does NOT:

  - Operate a server that receives, stores, or logs messages
  - Collect your IP address
  - Track your location
  - Use cookies for tracking
  - Use analytics services (no Google Analytics, no Mixpanel,
    no tracking pixels)
  - Create or maintain user accounts
  - Store any personally identifiable information


3. DATA STORED LOCALLY IN YOUR BROWSER
----------------------------------------------------------------
vMessage stores the following data in your browser's
localStorage only. This data never leaves your device.

  a) YOUR DISPLAY NAME
     Key: "vmessage-username"
     What: The display name you entered in the app.
     Why: So your name persists between visits.
     Cleared by: You, at any time, by clearing site data in
     your browser settings.
     Legacy compatibility note: older LAN Rooms builds may
     also leave "lan-rooms-username" in localStorage.

  b) TERMS ACCEPTANCE
     Key: "vmessage-tos-v1"
     What: A flag ("agreed") indicating you accepted the
     Terms of Service.
     Why: So the terms popup does not reappear on repeat
     visits.
     Cleared by: You, at any time, by clearing site data.
     Legacy compatibility note: older LAN Rooms builds may
     also leave "lan-rooms-tos-v1" in localStorage.

No other data is written to localStorage, sessionStorage,
IndexedDB, or cookies by this application.


4. DATA THAT PASSES THROUGH THIRD-PARTY INFRASTRUCTURE
----------------------------------------------------------------
When you use vMessage, a connection must be established
between your browser and other peers. This process involves:

  a) PEERJS SIGNALING SERVER
     During the initial handshake, your browser contacts
     PeerJS's public signaling server (0.peerjs.com or
     similar). This server facilitates the WebRTC connection
     and may transiently process your IP address and a
     randomly generated peer ID. PeerJS's own privacy policy
     governs this interaction. After the connection is
     established, the signaling server is no longer involved
     in any communication.

  b) STUN SERVERS
     WebRTC uses STUN servers to determine your public IP
     for NAT traversal. vMessage uses the default PeerJS
     STUN configuration. STUN servers see your IP address
     transiently during connection setup only.

  c) CDN-LOADED RESOURCES
     The application loads the following resources from CDNs
     at runtime:

       - PeerJS library from unpkg.com
       - Lucide icons from cdn.jsdelivr.net
       - Fonts from fonts.googleapis.com / fonts.gstatic.com

     These CDN providers may log standard HTTP request data
     (IP address, timestamp, user-agent) per their own
     policies. We have no access to this data.

We do not control and are not responsible for the data
practices of any of these third parties.


5. MESSAGE CONTENT
----------------------------------------------------------------
Messages are transmitted peer-to-peer via WebRTC data
channels. They do not pass through any server operated by
this project.

  - Public rooms: messages are transmitted over the
    encrypted WebRTC channel but are not additionally
    encrypted at the application layer.

  - Password-protected rooms: messages are encrypted with
    AES-256-GCM before transmission. Only peers who have
    derived the correct key from the room password can
    decrypt them.

In either case, messages are NOT stored anywhere after the
session ends. When a user disconnects, their messages are
gone. There is no message history, no logs, no backups.


6. EPHEMERAL CRYPTOGRAPHIC KEYS
----------------------------------------------------------------
On each page load, vMessage generates a fresh ECDSA P-256
keypair in your browser using the Web Crypto API. The private
key is marked non-extractable and exists only in memory for
the duration of your session. It is discarded when you close
or refresh the tab. It is never transmitted to anyone.


7. CHILDREN'S PRIVACY
----------------------------------------------------------------
vMessage is not directed at children under the age of 13
(or the applicable age in your jurisdiction). We do not
knowingly collect any information from minors. If you are
under the applicable age limit, please do not use this
Service.


8. YOUR RIGHTS & CONTROLS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Since we do not collect personal data on any server, there is
nothing for us to delete, export, or modify on your behalf.

Data stored in your browser (display name, ToS acceptance)
can be deleted at any time by:

  - Clearing site data in your browser settings
  - Opening browser DevTools → Application → Local Storage
    and deleting the relevant keys manually
